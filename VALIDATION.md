## Source of Truth Hierarchy

1. **Nicole's WIP History Import Excel files** (`Data-from-Nicole/`) — these are the ground truth for what override values *should* appear in the workbook.
2. **LylesWIP.WipJobData** — what the system *has stored*. Should match Nicole's files after `load_overrides.py` runs.
3. **Vista** (`10.112.11.8`) — system of record for job existence, contract structure, and financial actuals. Non-override columns come from Vista.
4. **Workbook Z columns** (`COLZOPsRev`, `COLZOPsCost`, `COLZGAAPRev`, `COLZGAAPCost`, `COLZOPsBonus`) — what LylesWIP *actually wrote* to the workbook at merge time. These are the validation checkpoints.

**Validation rule:** If Nicole's file has a non-zero override for a job, and the override was loaded into LylesWIP, and the workbook's Z column for that job equals Nicole's value (within $0.02), the pipeline is correct end-to-end.

## Known Edge Cases and Resolutions

### Sub-jobs: `56.1009.01` and `56.1010.01` (WML Div56)
Only 2 sub-jobs exist across all 4 companies in bJCJM. Sub-jobs have NO trailing dot in Vista.
Standard jobs: `56.1004.` (trailing dot). Sub-jobs: `56.1010.01` (no trailing dot).
LylesWIP stores sub-jobs WITH trailing dot (normalize_job adds it).
VBA fix (deployed Apr 2): `If Right(jobNum,1)<>"." Then jobNum=jobNum&"."` before dictionary lookup in all 3 Merge* functions.

`56.1009.01` — Hard Closed (MonthClosed=2024-12-01). May not appear in Dec 2025 WB unless cross-year reversal applies.
`56.1010.01` — Hard Closed (MonthClosed=2026-03-01 = future). Passes Dec 2025 WIP filter → appears in WB.

## Business Rules Confirmed by Nicole (March–April 2026)

| Rule | Source | Detail |
|------|--------|--------|
| WIP month filter | Nicole (Mar 31) | Ending Month = batch month; Beginning Month = blank (JTD). `@CutOffDate = EOMONTH(@Month)` |
| GAAP quarterly | Nicole confirmed | March, June, September, December only. Non-GAAP months: blank GAAP columns. |
| OPS threshold | Column mapping meeting | 30% complete → earned revenue = contract amount |
